ARC-DIR-02 Director Statement
"I look for the visual rhythm that already belongs to a piece. Then I build everything around it."

I'm drawn to projects that create a feeling before they explain themselves. Whether it's a game trailer, product film, commercial, or narrative piece, I'm chasing the same thing: emotional clarity and a point of view that belongs to the work, not just to the brief.

Direction and editing are not separate jobs for me. They're the same conversation at different stages. Directing shapes what happens. Editing shapes how it lands.

ARC-DIR-03 Operating Model
01 / Brief Find the audience, pressure, and point of view.

Clarify what the work needs to make someone feel, remember, or do.

02 / Concept Shape the creative language before the cut begins.

Define tone, reference, pacing, and the visual rules of the record.

03 / Direct Guide performance, camera, art, and production rhythm.

Keep the shoot focused on usable moments, not just coverage.

04 / Edit Build structure, tension, and emotional release.

Own the frame-to-frame decisions that make the idea land.

05 / Deliver Finish the piece for campaign, client, and platform needs.

Carry the record through final edit, cutdowns, and handoff.
