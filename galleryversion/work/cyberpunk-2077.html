Campaign film for CD Projekt Red's Cyberpunk 2077. The brief: make the audience feel Night City before the film explains itself. The world needed to push back at the viewer — not sell itself to them.

Challenge

Cyberpunk 2077 has one of the most visually dense worlds in games. Most of the audience already knows what it is. The challenge was finding the emotional frequency that makes them feel it again — not informed by it, but overwhelmed by it.

Execution

Pacing built to mirror the city: relentless, occasionally still, never resolved. Color pushed toward red and cyan in near-conflict. Sound design lives inside the cut, not around it.

Creative Approach

I built the piece around kinetic atmosphere rather than narrative logic. Every decision was about texture first: the abrasive color palette, the violence of the cuts, the way neon becomes oppressive at the right tempo. Night City should feel like it's resisting the viewer. The direction leans into that friction rather than resolving it. The edit doesn't conclude — it accumulates.
