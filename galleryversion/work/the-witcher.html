Cinematic film for CD Projekt Red's The Witcher. The brief was to build something that communicates the franchise's tonal identity — not an action showcase, but a portrait of a world that carries real moral weight.

Challenge

The Witcher carries a moral seriousness most game IP doesn't attempt. The creative challenge was preserving that gravity while producing something cinematically compelling — a piece that works for longtime fans and makes the world's emotional stakes legible to someone encountering it for the first time.

Execution

Pacing is deliberately slower than a conventional game trailer. Silence is editorial. Color favors muted earth tones broken by isolated warmth — firelight, copper, dying light. The score builds like a threat that hasn't landed yet.

Creative Approach

The piece is structured around character weight rather than event sequence. Geralt carries the world in every frame — the direction emphasizes that through timing and stillness rather than action. Atmosphere is the primary storytelling tool. The world should feel ancient and dangerous before a single word is spoken. Every editing decision deferred to mood over momentum. When those two things conflict in The Witcher's world, mood wins.
