Challenge

The campaign needed to drive holiday awareness without feeling like a generic promo card. It needed a character-led idea that could carry two near-identical versions: one for Black Friday and one for Cyber Monday.

Approach

The project was framed as the first appearance of Kairi and Pixel, not just a seasonal ad. The characters gave the sale message a story engine and created a foundation Corsair could revisit beyond the holiday window.

Overview
Campaign

The final campaign existed in two close variants: a Black Friday version and a Cyber Monday version. The core animation stayed nearly identical while the screen and promotional messaging shifted for each retail moment.

Creative note 01

The story had to survive the offer swap. If the character idea only worked for one sale message, it was too small.

Challenge

Holiday retail content can collapse into pure urgency: discount, date, logo, repeat. The stronger opportunity was to make the sale feel like an event inside a small animated universe.

Creative note 02

The campaign needed awareness, but the portfolio value is the character foundation underneath the promo.

Creative Solution

Kairi and Pixel gave the campaign a point of view. Kairi carried personality and emotional entry. Pixel added sidekick energy, digital magic, and motion potential. Together, they turned a sale beat into a character launch.

Creative note 03

The larger move was giving Corsair a pair of recurring characters that could outlive the holiday placement.

Character Development

Building Kairi From A Blank Page

Kairi started with no inherited template. I developed the name, personality, character direction, and visual concept so the campaign had a lead with warmth, curiosity, and enough specificity to feel ownable for Corsair.

The design reference pass was not about locking final animation production. It was about giving Pixune a clear character target: youthful, expressive, brand-safe, and flexible enough to lead future stories.

Creative note 04

Kairi needed to feel like a character first and a campaign device second. That changes every design choice.

Kairi Design Reference / Lead Character Direction
Pixel Development

A Sidekick Built For Motion

Pixel was developed as the digital companion: a small character with spark, timing, and screen-world logic. The sidekick role helped connect the human side of the story to the animated effects and product energy.

That relationship gave the animation team a practical foundation for action: touch points, screen transitions, reveal beats, and effects that could feel motivated instead of decorative.

Creative note 05

Pixel had to be useful in motion. The best sidekick can point, react, cause problems, solve problems, and make the frame move.

Pixel / Screen Contact Beat
Storyboarding & Animation Direction

Turning Promo Energy Into Action Beats

I built shot lists, storyboarded key beats, generated AI visual references, and provided motion, action, and effects ideas so Pixune had a clear sense of energy, staging, and campaign rhythm before animation production moved forward.

Creative note 06

The brief was awareness, but the board needed impact: a retail message arriving through character action instead of a static sale card.

Creative Direction
01

Developed the campaign premise around Kairi and Pixel entering the sale moment as characters, not mascots pasted onto a promotion. The story gave the retail message a reason to unfold.

Script / Text Direction

Wrote and shaped the text direction so the Black Friday and Cyber Monday versions could share the same structure while swapping the core promotional message cleanly.

03

Visual References

Generated AI references to communicate character tone, shot intent, effects language, and promotional staging before Pixune translated the direction into animation production.

Edit + Sound Effects

Edited the final video and added sound effects, shaping the pace, impact, and polish of the final campaign pieces after animation delivery.

Studio Collaboration
Pixune Studios Animation production partner

Pixune Studios handled animation production as Corsair's hired animation studio/vendor. They translated the campaign direction, boards, and revision feedback into the animated deliverables.

My Contribution Concept, character, story, direction, revision, edit

My work covered the creative foundation and delivery pass: Kairi, Pixel, story concept, script/text direction, shot lists, AI visual references, action and effects ideas, storyboard beats, feedback rounds, final edit, and sound effects.

Final Campaign Visuals
Final Campaign Delivery

The campaign shipped as two near-identical animated videos: one branded for Black Friday and one branded for Cyber Monday. The main difference was the screen and promotional messaging, which let the same character-led sequence serve both retail beats. Final videos were published across YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, and Corsair's website.

Creative note 07

The two-version structure worked because the campaign engine was not the text. It was the characters moving the viewer toward the offer.

Outcome

The most important result was not only a holiday animation campaign. It was the first use of Kairi and Pixel as potential recurring Corsair brand characters: a small campaign universe with names, personalities, visual direction, story logic, and animation language that could be extended into future brand moments.

Creative note 08

The campaign did its retail job, but the more durable value was creating characters Corsair could bring back.
